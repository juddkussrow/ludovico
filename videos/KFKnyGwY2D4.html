(2) Hand-select 4–9 keyframes showing distinct motion/poses/expressions (stored in separate folder). (3) Pack keyframes into sprite sheet via Sprite Sheet Packer (3×3 grid example). (4) Generate in A1111: load sprite sheet into text-to-image, enable 3 ControlNets (line art at 85 strength, open pose at high strength for facial expressions, reference optional at 0.5 strength), optional detailer off after testing softness, prompt engineering via Tyrant generator. (5) Split output sprite sheet via ezGIF cutter (512×512 tiles). (6) Rename AI keyframes to match original source frame numbers (manual F2 rename for each). (7) Load into EBSynth: source frames (original video chopped) in video folder, AI-styled keyframes in keyframes folder. (8) EBSynth settings: mapping 10 (follow source closely), flicker 1–2 (low jitter), diversity ~3500 (default), batch size small (~30 frames per keyframe to avoid degradation across large jumps). (9) Render, review output folder batches, regenerate batches with artifacts. (10) Compose in Premiere Pro with image sequence checked, adjust opacity, throw source overlay at reduced opacity to help consistency. (11) Final pass: Topaz Video AI to upscale to 1024×1024, 60fps interpolation, stabilization 2 passes at strength ~69 to reduce lighting flicker. Result: clean animation with minimal warping. Tradeoffs: interior vs exterior green often determines realism; more movement = harder to keep consistent.
