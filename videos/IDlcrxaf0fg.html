Proxy mesh workflow: manually model geometry (cubes/rectangles) aligned to splat, remesh in UE to add vertices, assign vertex color material (RGB→base color, R→roughness, G→emission, B→metallic), then mesh-paint roughness (black=reflective/wet, white=rough). Example: painting black streaks on street simulates wet pavement reflection. Final output: multi-pass render (no-light pass, red-light pass, blue-light pass) for compositing flexibility in Nuke + DaVinci Resolve. Test case: Toronto alleyway at night with actor overlay, green-screen tracked via Lightcraft Jetset, flashing police lights with splat relighting. Estimated normals can cause over-lighting on far surfaces; proxy mesh + normal from heightmap node mitigates this.
