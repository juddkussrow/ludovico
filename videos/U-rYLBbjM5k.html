Pixel space vs. latent space: most workflows operate in latent space (fast, low-cost but VAE decoding loses texture, text, edges, small objects). PixelDiT pioneers direct pixel-space generation but with mediocre results. PiD applies pixel-space diffusion to practical decoding: it converts latent data to high-resolution images (512→2048 for Flux one, 1024→4096 for Flux one). Sampling algorithm LCM has weaknesses (plastic texture, overly smooth details). Best use: images with poor original quality where faces, textures, background details are missing or unclear.
