SYNOPSIS: AI UGC is a service business requiring human creativity (strategy, storytelling, taste, brand direction) plus AI execution. Core revenue model: brands pay for scroll-stopping, on-brand video content cheaper and faster than traditional production. Creator pricing psychology: traditionally, quality climbs linearly with time (9% per hour). AI-only output reaches ~65% usable quality in 1 hour, then plateaus (lacks depth). Human+AI reaches 95% quality by 7–8 hours (best of both). Clients now pay dual cost: machine tokens/GPU/model fees + expert direction/QA/revisions. A content blueprint follows ATTENTION–RESONANCE–ACTION: (1) Hook with absurd or relatable opener, (2) Resonance: viewer recognizes their problem and invests time watching, (3) Action: CTA makes clicking easier than scrolling. Context is 99% human work; content generation is 1% AI. Three lip-sync production modes: (1) Video-to-Video (existing video → analyze mouth/jaw → overlay new audio → new lip-synced video; ideal for real people or AI-generated avatars already talking). Demonstrated with Synthetic UGC: selfie-style avatar, ElevenLabs voice generation with "direct speech" intonation control (user records desired delivery; AI mimics tone), upload to Synthetic UGC, run lip-sync. Result: believable mouth movement matching new audio. (2) Consistent Character Lip-Sync: image → Kling AI video generation (text-to-video, prompt: "lady talking to camera") → Synthetic UGC lip-sync. Requires clear face, sharp lips visible. Drawback: image quality vs. animation trade-offs. (3) Image-to-Lip-Sync (OmniHuman via Dremina/CapCut): upload image + audio → OmniHuman generates talking video directly. Faster than pipeline 2 but limited to 15 seconds per generation, 3-hour queue times (paid plan), exaggerated mouth movements. Synthetic UGC vs. OmniHuman: Dremina sharper/more detailed but overly dramatic; Synthetic less sharp but more usable out-of-box. B-roll generation: use Enhancor AI image generator with detailed prompts (e.g., "close-up very bad skin pimple", "person sitting on bed holding stomach"), select keywords + portrait aspect ratio + realism/angle settings → generate → download. Can enhance with Enhancor AI (fix skin texture) → upscale (detail boost) → re-enhance (recover smoothed skin details). Final workflow: AI avatar lip-synced video + B-roll images edited in CapCut (free) + CTA. Example script: cortisol health topic, pain points (dry skin, bloating, exhaustion), solutions (lemon balm tea, ashwagandha, breathing, caffeine cut), product pitch. Monetization: (1) Social media organic (free traffic via personal brand); (2) Fiverr/Upwork (marketplace discovery); (3) Cold email (low conversion for creator); (4) Niche AI accounts (3 posts/day, no personal appearance); (5) Clip platforms (pay per view + submission, e.g., "Clip and Cash"). Pricing model (author's business): hire freelancer $1,500 for 30 videos (~$50/video, $5–7 machine cost, ~86% freelancer margin). Charge client minimum $4,500 for 30 videos + 1-hour strategy call ($845/hour consulting = ~$3,000 net for author after freelancer payout, ~66% author margin). Freelancer earns $1,500. Community gigs available: free gig posts with per-view pay + 20% sales commission. Paid community ($monthly): deeper training in storytelling, content marketing, confetti UI workflows, weekly calls, job listings from top clients.
