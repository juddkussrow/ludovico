Gaussian Splatting represents scenes as collections of little Gaussians, enabling real-time rendering faster than real-time but with limited specular reflections and no fancy camera models (no fisheye, no rolling shutter). 3DGUT extends this by introducing secondary rays that bounce around the scene. Result: real-time rendering with high-quality reflections, refractions (glass), fisheye cameras, and rolling shutter—all running in real-time. Comparison: prior fisheye-enabled method showed artifacts and distortions; 3DGUT shows significant improvement especially near camera. Secondary work on subsurface scattering for Gaussian Splatting enables translucent object rendering (skin, marble, milk) with relighting capabilities—can relight objects and edit materials (skin→glass→wax). Source code released freely. Self-driving car training applications: support for distorted camera models and rolling shutter valuable for synthetic data generation.
