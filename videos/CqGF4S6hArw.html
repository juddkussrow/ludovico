Core problem: treating ChatGPT as a "write me a song" button produces robotic, repetitive output (echo, neon lights, shadows appearing obsessively). Solution: meta-prompting and workflow design. Techniques: (1) Music reference analysis—ask ChatGPT to act as musicologist/songwriter/producer/literary analyst analyzing Mr. Bright Side (Killers); extract rhythm, song structure, composition, emotional arc without copying lyrics (copyright boundary). (2) Artist analysis—same depth for artist style (Drake example); learn compositional patterns, lyric styles, aesthetics before prompting in that style. (3) Cliché awareness—make ChatGPT self-aware of its worst habits (echo, neon, shadows); add do-not-use instruction. (4) Backward writing technique—research technique first in ChatGPT, then use as brainstorm buddy for metaphors, imagery, emotions instead of full song generation. (5) Imagery/metaphor/emotion guidance—turn simple ideas into visual, narrative mini-stories to guide ChatGPT. (6) Direct meta-prompt—"how would you become expert at [task]?" Ask ChatGPT; use its answer as exact prompt. (7) Text transformation—feed existing poems, journal entries, famous quotes, articles, videos; ask to transform into original lyrics. (8) Merge technique—combine story of song A + genre/style of song B into original track (tested on drum-and-bass/bass-heavy with minimal lyrics). (9) Custom GPTs—navigate ChatGPT GPTs section, search lyrics + genre; use specialized models designed for lyric writing (usually superior to base ChatGPT). (10) ChatGPT Projects—create project, drag-drop workflow document, start workflow via "let's make a song" prompt.
