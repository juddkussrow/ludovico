Problem: good summaries are difficult; must be detailed and entity-centric without becoming opaque. CoD solution: iteratively add missing entities in 5 steps. Process: user uploads article; prompt requests increasingly concise entity-dense summaries. Each iteration identifies 3 informative entities missing from previous summary, writes new summary of identical length covering all prior entities plus new ones. By maintaining fixed word count (80 words, 4–5 sentences example) and adding entities, filler words are removed → higher density. Prompt specifies entity definition and output format. Density curve increases across 5 iterations. Demo: BBC Future article on older politicians (Biden, Trump, 2024 election, age/cognitive decline, age 65 threshold, super-agers, executive function, Barbara Sahakian, Mark Fisher, Nikki Haley, ageism, cognitive screening proposals). Summary 1: verbose, filler ("this article discusses..."). Summary 5: compressed, entity-packed ("2024 U.S. election featuring Biden and Trump Sparks debates...Mark Fisher identifies age 65...Barbara Sahakian..."). Result: denser abstraction, more fusional (entity relationships), less lead bias vs. zero-shot GPT-4. Use case: batch article summarization for research, content curation, dense knowledge extraction. Audio playback of summary 5 tested via 11 Labs. VERDICT: Chain of Density is a practical, cost-effective prompt-engineering technique for achieving high-quality dense summaries without fine-tuning or special models.
