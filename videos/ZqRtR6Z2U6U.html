Cortical Labs · no reliable link · software · mentioned SYNOPSIS: CL1 is a self-contained biocomputing system by Cortical Labs housing 200,000 living human neurons (vs. 1 million in predecessor DishBrain). Neurons cultured from induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs, Nobel Prize technique): non-invasive donation (skin swab/blood draw) reprogrammed via transcription factors to blank-slate stem cells, then differentiated into neurons. Neurons grown as 2D monolayer on high-density microelectrode array (HDMEA)—glass/metal substrate with embedded microscopic electrodes directly contacting cells. Two-way communication: system stimulates neurons with electrical pulses (frequency/amplitude/timing encode game state) and records spiking activity at sub-millisecond resolution (40,000 samples/sec). Life-support: microfluidic perfusion circuit delivers nutrient-rich synthetic bloodstream (glucose, oxygen); microscopic filtration removes waste (kidney function); temperature controlled to 37°C; automated gas mixing manages O2/CO2. Lifespan: ~6 months. Form factor: self-contained, server-rack compatible (no external life support). Game encoding: Doom visual data → ray-casting geometry detection → electrical pulse patterns (enemy left side = left electrode stimulation, wall = different pattern, kill = reward signal). Neurons respond with spikes decoded back to game commands (fire, move, turn). Learning mechanism: free-energy principle—biological systems minimize surprise. Useful actions → smooth, predictable signals (desired by neurons). Bad actions → chaotic electrical noise (neurons reorganize to reduce chaos, thus learning). Neuron count comparison: human brain ~86 billion, fruit fly ~100,000, C. elegans ~200 (same as CL1 nuclei-count, but different complexity). CL1 learns Doom gameplay in hours vs. GPT-style models requiring millions of iterations. Energy: human brain ~20 watts (light bulb power); modern AI data centers consuming massive power grids. Potential: living neurons excel at real-time physical control (Moravec's Paradox—tasks hard for humans are easy for AI; tasks easy for humans are hard for robots; biology evolved for real-world environments). Ethics: Researchers argue CL1 not conscious (too small, lacks brain structures for emotion/memory/pain); scaling question unresolved.
