Clear Angle's Volumetric Capture Rig (VCR): half-dome with 40 genlocked ML cameras, integrated neutral LED lighting, 3m×3m×3m capture volume. Captures 1–3 individual performers with separation to avoid occlusion, or small groups interacting (dancing, pushing) for realistic crowd behavior. 4D splats animated with no traditional rigging/animation required. Challenges solved: temporal stability (frame-to-frame jitter) via denoising; point-cloud inconsistency via ML-based image segmentation (jacket, trousers, shirt, hair, skin isolated per angle) → cryptomatte export for color variation in compositing; lack of re-lightable surface data via training pipeline that produces high-definition surface normals, enabling relighting inside any CG scene with production renderer (Karma XPU). Splats cast and receive shadows, integrate with stadium/CG lighting. Render times: ~20min for 2K on GPU-enabled machine. Camera distance tested: ≈2m from character (three-quarters of screen height). Workflow: capture → splat processing (Clear Angle) → USD sprite layout in Nuke (for rapid editorial/director approval before full splat processing) → metadata export for re-execution. Splat limitations acknowledged: view-dependency ignored in re-lighting workflow (trade-off for freedom); temporal consistency holds for 60–120s+ captures (no drift like generative AI); multi-step memory shorter than Genie 3. Future applications: digital doubles (superhero flight), long-duration performances on wires. Supervisor/producer feedback positive; perceived as solution to crowd/double production pain points. Industry comparison: splats offer photorealism + control (unlike generative video), deterministic output without hallucinations or decoherence (unlike GenAI video), art-directable (unlike card-based crowds).
