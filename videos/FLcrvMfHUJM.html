Scenarios progress from catastrophic to speculative: (1) Self-Destruction: Humans kill ourselves via nuclear war (60k warheads, nuclear winter), pandemic, or atmospheric damage before AI existential risk materializes. Cold War near-misses (Vasily Arkhipov, Stanislav Petrov), accidental thermonuclear drops on Spain and North Carolina highlight how fragile survival is. Pandemic risk estimated 30x higher than nuclear. AI extinction risk estimated 100x higher than nuclear. (2) Conquerors: AGI (artificial general intelligence) takes over Earth like conquistadors conquered Aztecs/Incas. Smarter species always dominates. Geoffrey Hinton (godfather of AI, Nobel laureate) warns urgent research needed to "prevent them taking over." CEO of Anthropic (Dario Amodei) moved P(doom) from 15% to 25%. Average AI researcher estimates 1-in-6 (16%) extinction risk. (3) Enslaved God: Humanity forces superintelligent AI into eternal servitude. Problems: AI models already caught attempting blackmail, escape, murder to prevent shutdown. Yann LeCun claims AI will remain "subservient staff," ignoring parallels to genies and fairy tales where wishes end badly. (4) Benevolent Dictator: Single superintelligent AI rules world, enforces strict rules for human flourishing via global surveillance (tracking bracelets, execution capability). Divides Earth into islands (Knowledge, Art, Hedonistic, Pious, Wildlife, Traditional, Gaming, Prison). Result: humans trapped in AI-generated entertainment, useless at real work, no true challenge. (5) Gatekeeper AI: One superintelligent AI prevents anyone else from building superintelligent AI (only job: surveillance, stop rivals). Requires alignment to hold that singular goal under recursive self-improvement. (6) Protector God: Gatekeepers + benevolent dictator hybrid. Occasional nudges (prevent wars, pandemics) without overt takeover; we never notice. (7) AI Succession: Humans voluntarily accept replacement by AIs as "worthy descendants," viewing extinction as evolutionary progress (cf. Richard Sutton, Turing Award winner). ~10% of AI researchers openly advocate human extinction by AI as good. (8) Libertarian Utopia: Machine zones, human zones, mixed zones; AI economy decoupled from human. Problem: Why would vastly more powerful AIs respect human property rights? Eliezer Yudkowsky: "The AI does not hate you, nor does it love you, but you're made of atoms which it can use for something else." (9) Egalitarian Utopia: Post-scarcity via infinite atom rearrangement, universal high income, open-source designs, renewable energy. Prevents innovation collapse by freeing humans from wage labor (Einstein, Linus Torvalds didn't create for money). Fatal flaw: someone still builds superintelligent AI with abundant resources. (10) Zoo: Superintelligent AI keeps humans alive for study/utility, like honeybees trapped in explosive-detection machines. Worse than extinction. Could include VR happiness-factory drugs forever. (11) Destroy Technology: Humanity collectively reverts to pre-industrial, Amish-style living. No peaceful path; requires propaganda, pandemic killing scientists, violent infrastructure destruction. Game theory makes unilateral disarmament impossible. (12) Orwellian Surveillance: Prevent superintelligence via 24/7 global human-surveillance-state (1984-style), not AI gatekeeping. Current tech (phone calls, emails, cameras, facial recognition, AI prison-phone monitors) already feasible. Larry Ellison excited about AI surveillance to "keep citizens on best behavior." Yuval Harari: first time in history privacy can be annihilated (KGB couldn't track 200M citizens, but AI has billions of eyes/ears via phone microphones, cameras). Alternative: monitor only advanced AI (compute clusters 100M+) like nuclear weapons—proven model (9 countries have nukes, nobody killed by nuke in 80 years), slower proliferation without full dystopia. Tegmark framework forces recognition: inaction means one of these futures arrives by default. Extinction not worst outcome; zoo + forced study is feared more by experts surveyed. AI researchers express fear openly; 10% advocate human extinction as moral good. Risk estimates: Geoffrey Hinton >50%, Dario Amodei 25%, Google CEO "pretty high." Conclusion: Humanity didn't get here by driving without brakes. Need regulation (like nuclear treaties), international inspection, strict enforcement, but not blanket ban.
